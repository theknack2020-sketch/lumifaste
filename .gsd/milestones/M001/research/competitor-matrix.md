# Intermittent Fasting Apps — Competitive Analysis Matrix (US App Store, March 2026)

> Research compiled March 22, 2026 from App Store listings, review sites, and expert roundups.
> Ratings and review counts are approximate and change frequently.

---

## Summary

The intermittent fasting app market is mature and crowded, with **20+ serious contenders** on the US App Store. The space divides into three tiers:

1. **Dominant players** (Zero, Fastic, Simple, BodyFast) — millions of downloads, heavy marketing spend, aggressive subscription pricing ($30–$70/yr)
2. **Strong mid-tier** (DoFasting, YAZIO, Life Fasting, Lasta, Fasted) — differentiated positioning, growing user bases
3. **Niche/minimal** (Fastient, FastHabit, Vora, Ate, Window, Easy Fast, Serene) — smaller but loyal audiences, often better free tiers

**Key market dynamics:**
- Nearly all apps are freemium with subscriptions ranging from $5/yr to $70/yr
- AI features (food scanning, chatbot coaching) are the 2025–2026 battleground
- Community/social features differentiate Fastic and Life from timer-only apps
- User backlash against aggressive paywalls is a consistent complaint across the market
- The "Leap Fitness Group" generic fasting tracker has massive download numbers from ASO/ad spend

## Key Insights & Market Gaps

### 1. Subscription Fatigue is Real
The #1 complaint across ALL apps is aggressive paywalling. Users frequently rate apps 1-star specifically because of subscription pressure. Apps with generous free tiers (Easy Fast, Fasted, FastHabit) earn outsized loyalty relative to their feature sets.

### 2. AI is the Current Arms Race
Fastic's "Fasty" chatbot, Simple's AI coach, and photo-based food scanning are the 2025–2026 differentiators. Zero and BodyFast are behind on AI integration.

### 3. Community is Underserved
Only Fastic (buddies/challenges) and Life (circles) have meaningful social features. Most apps are solo experiences. There's an opportunity for deeper social accountability.

### 4. Apple Watch & Widgets are Rare
Despite high demand, only Zero, FastHabit, and Easy Fast have robust Apple Watch apps. Live Activities/Dynamic Island support is extremely rare (only Easy Fast).

### 5. Educational Content Differentiates
Zero's science-backed content library is a major retention driver. Most competitors have thin or no educational content.

### 6. The "Timer-Only" Segment is Dying
Pure timer apps (Window, Serene) are losing ground. Users expect at minimum: timer + weight tracking + basic stats. The floor keeps rising.

### 7. Privacy-First is a Niche Opportunity
Easy Fast's "no account, no data collection, no ads" positioning resonates strongly with a privacy-conscious segment that no major player addresses.

### 8. Life Fasting Tracker is Declining
Once a top-3 app, Life's development has stalled. Its social circles feature was pioneering but hasn't evolved. Users are migrating to Fastic and Zero.
