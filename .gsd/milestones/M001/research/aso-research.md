# ASO Research: Fasting Apps

> **Date:** March 22, 2026
> **Category:** Health & Fitness → Fasting / Weight Loss
> **Stores:** Apple App Store (primary), Google Play (secondary)

---

## Summary

The fasting app market is a high-revenue, high-competition segment within Health & Fitness. The top 5 results for "intermittent fasting" in the US App Store generate an estimated $1.5M/month in net revenue. The category is dominated by Zero, Simple, Fastic, BodyFast, and Lasta, but meaningful opportunities exist in long-tail keywords, underserved localizations, and differentiated positioning. Search remains the dominant discovery channel — roughly 70% of App Store visits use search, and 65% of downloads happen directly after a search.

### 1.2 Keyword Strategy Tiers

**Tier 1 — Must-Have (Title + Subtitle)**
- `intermittent fasting`, `fasting`, `tracker`
- These define the category. Must appear in title or subtitle.

**Tier 2 — High Value (Subtitle + Keyword Field)**
- `fasting timer`, `fasting app`, `weight loss`, `health`
- Strong intent signals. Place in subtitle or keyword field.

**Tier 3 — Long-Tail Opportunities (Keyword Field)**
- `16:8 fasting`, `OMAD`, `autophagy`, `time restricted eating`, `fat burning`, `ketosis`, `fasting schedule`, `eating window`, `fasting reminders`, `water fasting`, `fasting for beginners`, `fasting challenge`
- Lower volume but much lower difficulty. Realistic to rank top-5 for a new app.

**Tier 4 — Adjacent / Expansion**
- `calorie tracker`, `meal planner`, `carb manager`, `weight tracker`, `body measurement`, `habit tracker`, `wellness`, `metabolism`
- Only pursue if the app genuinely offers these features.

### 2.2 Competitive Gaps & Opportunities

- **Zero's vulnerability:** "Intermittent" is NOT in Zero's title or subtitle — it's only in the keyword field. This makes Zero's #1 ranking fragile. A competitor with "intermittent fasting" in the title + strong ratings velocity could overtake it.
- **BodyFast's cross-category play:** BodyFast uniquely ranks for "carb manager" and "carb tracker" — keywords Zero doesn't target. This shows opportunity in adjacent nutritional keywords.
- **Brandless apps ranking well:** Several brandless apps using keyword-stuffed names rank in top 10. While not recommended long-term, it proves the algorithm still weights title keywords heavily.
- **In-app events underused:** Zero doesn't use in-app events; competitors do. Events show in search results and the Today tab, providing free incremental impressions.

## 4. Metadata Best Practices

### 4.1 Apple App Store — Title (30 characters max)

**Format:** `BrandName: Primary Keyword`

**Recommended:**
```
[YourBrand]: Intermittent Fasting
```

**Why:** The title carries the highest keyword weight. "Intermittent fasting" in the title is the single strongest ranking signal for the category's head term. Including a keyword in the title can boost ranking ~10% or more vs. not including it.

**Rules:**
- Don't include "app" (Apple indexes it automatically)
- Don't include "#1" or "best" (against guidelines, looks spammy)
- Don't add year numbers ("2026")

### 4.2 Apple App Store — Subtitle (30 characters max)

**Format:** `Verb + Benefit + Secondary Keyword`

**Recommended options:**
```
Fasting Timer & Weight Tracker
```
```
Track Fasts, Lose Weight
```
```
Fasting Timer & Health Tracker
```

**Why:** Subtitle is the second-strongest ranking signal. Use it to capture secondary keywords not in the title. The subtitle should also read naturally — it's visible to users and affects tap-through rate.

### 4.3 Apple App Store — Keyword Field (100 characters max)

**Rules:**
- Separate with commas, NO spaces after commas
- Don't repeat words already in Title or Subtitle (Apple combines tokens across fields)
- Don't use "app", "the", "a", "an" (stop words, auto-indexed)
- Use singular forms (Apple matches plurals automatically)
- Don't use competitor brand names (Apple may reject)
- Avoid long phrases — break into individual words that Apple recombines

**Example keyword field (98 characters):**
```
16:8,OMAD,autophagy,ketosis,diet,calorie,meal,plan,schedule,reminder,streak,journal,beginner,body
```

**Rationale:** This covers:
- Protocol terms: 16:8, OMAD
- Science terms: autophagy, ketosis
- Adjacent features: diet, calorie, meal, plan
- Functional terms: schedule, reminder, streak, journal
- Audience terms: beginner
- Measurement: body

Combined with title ("intermittent fasting") and subtitle ("fasting timer", "weight", "tracker" or "health"), this creates keyword combinations like:
- "intermittent fasting timer"
- "fasting schedule"
- "fasting for beginners"
- "OMAD fasting tracker"
- "autophagy fasting"
- "16:8 fasting app"
- "fasting diet plan"
- "fasting calorie tracker"

### 4.4 Google Play — Key Differences

- Title: 30 characters (same as iOS)
- Short description: 80 characters (indexed, high weight — treat like iOS subtitle + more)
- Full description: 4,000 characters (indexed! Unlike iOS. Include keywords naturally ~3–5 times)
- No hidden keyword field — all keywords must be in visible text
- Keyword density matters but stuffing is penalized
- Description should read naturally with bullet points for key features

### 4.5 Apple App Store — Description (4,000 characters, NOT indexed)

The iOS description doesn't affect search rankings. Use it purely for conversion:
- Lead with the user's problem and your solution
- Bullet-point key features
- Include social proof (download count, rating, press mentions)
- End with a clear CTA: "Download now and start your first fast today"
- Use Promotional Text (170 chars, editable without app update) for seasonal messaging

## 5. Screenshot & Preview Video Best Practices

### 5.1 Screenshot Strategy for Fasting/Health Apps

**Critical rule: ~90% of users don't scroll past the 3rd screenshot.** The first three images decide whether your app gets installed. Design time should be disproportionately spent on screenshots 1–3.

#### Screenshot Sequence (Recommended for Fasting Apps)

| # | Content | Caption Formula |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Fasting timer — active fast with countdown, showing body status/phase | **"Start fasting. See results."** — Hook with the core value prop |
| **2** | Progress dashboard — weight chart, streak counter, fasting history | **"Track every fast. Watch your progress."** — Show data/proof |
| **3** | Social proof / result — weight lost stat, streak milestone, or user testimonial | **"Join 1M+ fasters losing weight"** — Build trust |
| **4** | Fasting plans — selection of 16:8, 18:6, OMAD, custom | **"Pick your plan. We'll guide you."** — Show flexibility |
| **5** | Meal/calorie logging or food scanner (if applicable) | **"Know what you eat. Eat smarter."** — Show depth |
| **6** | Apple Watch / widget integration | **"Fast from your wrist."** — Device integration = premium signal |
| **7** | Insights / educational content / body status phases | **"Understand your body's fasting zones"** — Science credibility |
| **8–10** | Dark mode view, notifications/reminders, community features | Supporting features |

#### Design Principles

- **Lead with outcomes, not features.** "Lose weight with fasting" not "Advanced timer with customizable intervals."
- **Caption formula:** Verb + Benefit + Result (3–5 words). Must be readable at thumbnail size.
- **Use modern device frames** (iPhone 16 Pro). Outdated frames signal an abandoned app.
- **Color palette:** Green and blue build trust and credibility in health apps. Avoid overly aggressive reds.
- **Show realistic data** — not placeholder text. A timer showing "14h 32m" with a realistic name feels authentic.
- **Dark mode:** With 50%+ users using dark mode, show at least one dark-mode screenshot.
- **Panoramic technique:** Design a continuous background that flows across screenshots 1–3 to create visual tension that encourages scrolling.
- **Localize visuals, not just text:** Adapt cultural context, not just translate captions. Japanese design preferences differ significantly from Western ones.

### 5.2 App Preview Video Best Practices

- **Length:** 15–30 seconds max. Apple allows up to 30s. Front-load value in the first 5 seconds.
- **Auto-play consideration:** Videos auto-play without sound on Wi-Fi. Design for silent viewing with text overlays.
- **Structure:**
  1. (0–5s) Hook: Show the fasting timer starting with one tap
  2. (5–15s) Value: Show progress tracking, weight going down, streak building
  3. (15–25s) Depth: Show plans, insights, Apple Watch
  4. (25–30s) CTA: "Start your free fast today"
- **Format:** H.264 or ProRes 422, matching device resolution exactly (1290×2796 for 6.9" iPhone)
- **For health apps specifically:** Show a transformation narrative (not before/after body photos — that can trigger guideline issues) but data transformation: "Day 1 → Day 30" with improving metrics.

### 5.3 Technical Specifications (2026)

| Device | Resolution (Portrait) | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| iPhone 6.9" (16 Pro Max) | 1290 × 2796 px | ✅ Required for new/updated apps |
| iPhone 6.7" | 1290 × 2796 px | Covered by 6.9" |
| iPhone 6.1" | 1179 × 2556 px | Auto-scaled from 6.9" |
| iPad 13" | 2064 × 2752 px | ✅ Required if iPad supported |
| Apple Watch | 410 × 502 px (Series 10) | Recommended if Watch app exists |

- Format: `.png` or `.jpg` — RGB color space, flattened
- Count: 1–10 per device class per localization
- Must reflect actual app UI (no stock photos, no 3D renders without app interface)

## 6. Localization Opportunities

### 6.1 Why Localize?

On iOS, additional locale metadata dramatically expands reach. Apple allows you to distribute keywords across locale-specific fields, meaning each localization gives you an additional 160+ characters of indexed metadata (title + subtitle + keywords). Even if the app UI is English-only, localizing metadata captures searches in other languages.

### 6.2 Underserved Markets for Fasting Apps

| Market | Language | Opportunity | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Arabic-speaking** (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Egypt) | Arabic | 🔥 **Very High** | Ramadan drives massive seasonal demand. Most fasting apps have poor or no Arabic localization. Religious fasting (sunrise/sunset) is underserved. 400M+ Arabic speakers. |
| **Turkish** | Turkish | 🔥 **High** | Large Muslim population with Ramadan fasting culture. Growing smartphone market. Very few localized fasting apps. |
| **Indonesian / Malay** | Bahasa Indonesia / Malay | 🔥 **High** | Indonesia = world's largest Muslim population (230M+). Massive Ramadan demand. Very few quality localized fasting apps. |
| **German** (DACH) | German | **High** | Strong health-conscious market. BodyFast is German-origin and dominates. Opportunity to compete with better localization. High App Store spend per user. |
| **Japanese** | Japanese | **High** | Health-conscious culture, high ARPU. One-time purchases preferred over subscriptions. "断食" (danjiki) and "ファスティング" (fasutingu) are distinct search terms. Few quality localized options. |
| **Korean** | Korean | **Medium-High** | Growing diet culture, high smartphone penetration. "간헐적 단식" (intermittent fasting) growing in search. |
| **Portuguese** (Brazil) | Portuguese | **Medium-High** | Large market (210M+ population), growing health app adoption. Few Portuguese-localized fasting apps. |
| **Spanish** (LATAM + Spain) | Spanish | **Medium** | 500M+ speakers. Some competitors are localized, but keyword optimization in Spanish is often poor. Different terms across regions (Spain vs. Mexico vs. Argentina). |
| **Hindi** | Hindi | **Medium** | 600M+ speakers. India has strong fasting traditions (religious and health). Extremely underserved in App Store despite massive population. |
| **French** | French | **Medium** | France has a "jeûne intermittent" trend. Moderate competition. Useful for covering France, Belgium, parts of Africa. |
| **Polish** | Polish | **Low-Medium** | Growing health market. Very few competitors localize to Polish. Low-effort, moderate-reward. |

### 6.3 Localization Strategy Recommendations

1. **Start with Arabic + Turkish + Indonesian** — massive Ramadan-driven seasonal spikes with almost no quality competition. These markets alone could add significant download volume during March–April each year.

2. **German is strategic** — DACH has the highest per-user revenue in Europe. BodyFast's home territory, but beatable with better UX + ASO.

3. **Don't just translate — localize keywords independently.** Each market has different search terms. "Intermittent fasting" in Japanese could be "断食" (danjiki = fasting) or "ファスティング" (fasutingu = loan word). Research each market's actual search terms with an ASO tool.

4. **Leverage Apple's cross-localization:** On iOS, some locales share keyword fields (e.g., US English metadata may surface in UK, Australia, Canada). Strategically use UK English locale to add more keyword capacity without changing the primary listing.

5. **Cultural adaptation for screenshots:** Don't just translate screenshot captions. Adapt the visual style — e.g., Japanese users respond to different aesthetics than Western users. Ramadan-themed screenshots for Arabic markets during fasting season.

6. **Seasonal localized in-app events:** Create Ramadan-specific in-app events with Arabic/Turkish/Indonesian metadata during the fasting month. These surface on the Today tab and in search results.

## 7. Actionable Recommendations Summary

### Quick Wins (Week 1)
- [ ] Place "intermittent fasting" in app title
- [ ] Maximize keyword field with long-tail terms (no duplicates from title/subtitle)
- [ ] Create/update first 3 screenshots with outcome-focused captions
- [ ] Set up in-app events for upcoming seasonal moments
- [ ] Request ratings after positive moments (completing a fast, hitting a streak)

### Medium-Term (Month 1–2)
- [ ] Localize metadata for Arabic, Turkish, and Indonesian (even if app UI stays English)
- [ ] Localize metadata for German and Japanese
- [ ] Create Custom Product Pages (CPPs) targeting different keyword clusters
- [ ] Design and submit App Preview video (15–30s, silent-friendly)
- [ ] Run Apple Search Ads discovery campaigns to find converting keywords you're missing
- [ ] A/B test screenshot order and caption copy

### Ongoing Optimization
- [ ] Review keyword rankings weekly — focus on positions 6–15 (push to top 5)
- [ ] Update metadata monthly based on performance data
- [ ] Refresh screenshots quarterly or when major features launch
- [ ] Monitor competitor metadata changes (use AppTweak/MobileAction alerts)
- [ ] Publish seasonal in-app events (New Year, Ramadan, Summer, Back-to-School)
- [ ] Respond to all negative reviews (improves ratings + can surface new keyword ideas from user language)
