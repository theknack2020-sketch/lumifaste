# Fasting App User Complaints — Deep Dive Research

> **Sources:** App Store reviews, Google Play reviews, Trustpilot, Apple Community forums, Reddit (r/intermittentfasting, r/fasting), Medium, review sites (Innerbody, TechRadar, Fortune, The Dolce Way)
>
> **Apps analyzed:** Zero, Fastic, DoFasting, BodyFast, Simple, Fastyle, Fastin, FastHabit, Easy Fast, Certified Fasting, GoFasting, and others
>
> **Date:** March 2026

---

## Summary

Fasting app users are overwhelmingly frustrated by aggressive monetization tactics (paywalled timers, sneaky subscriptions, intrusive ads), bloated UX that turns a simple timer into a complex wellness platform, and basic technical failures (broken streaks, sync bugs, crashes after updates). The gap between what users *want* — a clean, reliable fasting timer with basic tracking — and what apps *deliver* — subscription-gated feature mazes — is the single biggest opportunity in the space.

## TOP 15 COMPLAINTS BY CATEGORY

---

### Category 1: Monetization Complaints

#### #1. Basic Features Paywalled (Timers, Fasting Schedules)
**Severity: Critical — Most common complaint across all apps**

Users are furious that fundamental fasting functionality — setting a custom fasting window — is locked behind premium subscriptions. This is the equivalent of a calculator app charging you to use the equals sign.

> *"This app takes you through a 10-minute onboarding with 30+ questions, only to reveal that you can't freely adjust your fasting/eating hours unless you upgrade to Premium. That's the most fundamental part of intermittent fasting, and it's locked behind a paywall."*
> — GoFasting, Google Play review

> *"It sucks that some fasting hours are locked behind a paywall. I like the 18:6 schedule, but I can't have that unless I pay."*
> — GoFasting, Google Play review

> *"The free version is so limited it is essentially useless. I was looking for a replacement for Life fasting that shut down, but sadly this isn't it."*
> — Google Play review, Fasting app by Leap Fitness Group

> *"Super easy to use and user-friendly, but it's unfortunate that so much with the insights and all that are locked behind a paywall."*
> — Fastin, Google Play review

**Apps cited:** GoFasting, Zero (Plus), BodyFast, Fastin, Fastyle, most premium-tier apps

---

#### #2. Expensive Subscriptions ($70-$80/year) for a Timer
**Severity: High**

Users cannot justify $70/year for what they perceive as a glorified countdown timer. Multiple review sites and user communities express this sentiment.

> *"Though the app offers a free seven-day trial, it costs $69.99 annually, which is pricey. If you're simply using the app to time your fasting and eating windows, you're better off setting a timer on your smartphone."*
> — Fortune magazine review of Zero

> *"Zero has 50 features you'll never touch. I switched to something simpler and actually stuck with fasting."*
> — The Dolce Way (review site)

> *"$70/year for a timer with features that free apps include? That's a tough sell."*
> — The Dolce Way comparative review

> *"Is that the trick, I have to pay for it again for me to even use the basic fasting timer? That's not right!"*
> — Zero, App Store review (809-day streak user)

**Apps cited:** Zero ($69.99/yr), Simple ($79.20/yr), DoFasting (~$138/yr), BodyFast (coach tier)

---

#### #3. Sneaky Subscriptions / Impossible to Cancel / Charged After Cancellation
**Severity: Critical**

This pattern appears across multiple apps — users report being charged after cancellation, hidden billing through PayPal bypassing App Store cancellation, and needing multiple cancellation attempts.

> *"I had to cancel three times before my cancellation was accepted."*
> — DoFasting, Trustpilot review

> *"This company is a scam... they keep charging you subscription fee even after cancellation. In my case I cancelled in 2020 and they are again charging me in 2024 and 2025."*
> — DoFasting, Trustpilot review

> *"The Simple App charged my PayPal account tied to my Apple ID $1 on the day I downloaded the app and then $79.20 on the 7th day (the day free trial ended). Apple will not help."*
> — Apple Community forum (Simple app)

> *"I have been charged $79.20 three times after I canceled! Simple is no longer responding to my emails."*
> — Apple Community forum

> *"You cancel the subscription, yet they keep charging money. You send an email to their support — an AI replies, and no action follows."*
> — Certified Fasting, Trustpilot review

**Apps cited:** DoFasting, Simple, Certified Fasting, Fastyle

---

#### #4. Intrusive Ads That Break the App Experience
**Severity: High**

Free-tier users report ads so aggressive they make apps unusable — full-screen interstitials on every screen transition, loud audio ads, and freezing during ad dismissal.

> *"Every new screen I went into seemed to have a pop up ad. I would have to wait for what seemed like forever to be able to skip the ad... Even when I tried to click out of some of them they would freeze the app or it would bring up the App Store to download whatever was being advertised."*
> — Fasting + Intermittent Fasting, App Store review

> *"While navigating the app, more often than not, I end up closing it due to the ads that interfere with its use. Also, the loud sounds that pop up with some of the ads are another reason I choose not to use this app very often."*
> — Fastin, Google Play review

> *"UPDATE: Deleted, the ads interfered with user experience."*
> — Fastin, Google Play review (follow-up)

> *"The ones I've used in the past now are updated with all kinds of ads and unnecessary features (Fastic...)."*
> — App Store review comparing apps

**Apps cited:** Fastin, Fastic, multiple free-tier fasting apps

---

#### #5. Bait-and-Switch: Advertised as "Free" but Requires Payment
**Severity: High**

Ads and App Store listings promise free functionality but reveal mandatory payment after a lengthy onboarding quiz, wasting users' time and eroding trust.

> *"Every ad says this is a free app but once you get through the quiz you can't do anything without paying. I get that these apps need to make money but customers shouldn't have to pay to find out if an app works for them."*
> — Fasting For Weight Loss App, App Store review

> *"First I downloaded app because an influencer classified it as a free app. When starting tutorial proceeded it was so smooth to sign a $80 per year plan."*
> — Fastic, Trustpilot review

> *"I don't trust companies who do the bait and switch tactics, makes me not trust them."*
> — App Store review

**Apps cited:** DoFasting, Fasting For Weight Loss (SoonFasting), Fastic, Certified Fasting

### Category 2: UX Complaints

#### #6. Feature Bloat — Too Many Features, Not Enough Simplicity
**Severity: High — Repeatedly cited as reason users switch to simpler apps**

Users who just want a fasting timer are overwhelmed by calorie counters, meal plans, workout libraries, coaching programs, social features, and article feeds. The core job — tracking a fast — gets buried.

> *"I love how simple, straightforward, and easy to use this app is! I've used other apps that are filled with ads, extra articles I'm not interested in, or demanding that I set up a whole user profile and turn the experience into ANOTHER social media experience."*
> — Easy Fast, App Store review (switching from competitors)

> *"And for God's sake, no upsell to some coaching program. They leave you alone. They don't bother you to join additional programs or unnecessary features like most of the other apps in this category do."*
> — Fasty (Intermittent Fasting Tracker), App Store review

> *"It's not cluttered with tips, 'inspiration', or weird chat groups."*
> — FastHabit, App Store review

> *"It has a nicely designed interface and is not overloaded with features. It's a great model for app design."*
> — Easy Fast review (contrasting with competitors)

**Apps cited:** Zero, Fastic, DoFasting, BodyFast — praised alternatives: Easy Fast, FastHabit, Fasty

---

#### #7. Lengthy Onboarding / Forced Account Creation
**Severity: Medium-High**

Users report 10+ minute onboarding flows with 30+ questions before they can even start a timer, plus mandatory account sign-ups that feel unnecessary for a local timer app.

> *"This app takes you through a 10-minute onboarding with 30+ questions... At that point, you might as well make the app paid instead of wasting people's time."*
> — GoFasting, Google Play review

> *"I built Easy Fast because I was tired of bloated apps that require logins just to set a timer."*
> — Easy Fast developer (Sam), App Store description

> *"It's perfect because it's not too overwhelming with information, doesn't bombard you with pop-ups or unwanted notifications."*
> — App Store review (praising simple alternatives)

**Apps cited:** GoFasting, DoFasting, Fastic, Simple — nearly all quiz-based apps

---

#### #8. Can't Edit Past Fasts / Inflexible Timer
**Severity: Medium**

Users who forget to start/stop their timer can't retroactively correct their data, making their history inaccurate and destroying motivation.

> *"I really wish this had the ability to manually change my start and stop fasting time. If I forget to log it in, I can't go back and change the time. So I'm stuck with times that aren't accurate."*
> — GoFasting, Google Play review

> *"Maybe I'm doing it wrong, but I'd give this a five star rating if I could just enter my start and planned end times. Trying to manipulate by days or hours is FRUSTRATING because it isn't exact."*
> — Fasting + Intermittent Fasting, App Store review

> *"If I want to set a 42 hour planned fast, the closest I can plan is 48 hours and then I hate the graph. I can edit the end time AFTER the fast, why can't I edit it during the fast?!"*
> — App Store review

**Apps cited:** GoFasting, Fasting+, various apps with rigid timer UIs

### Category 3: Accuracy Complaints

#### #9. Questionable "Body Status" / Fasting Zone Science
**Severity: Medium**

Apps show elaborate fasting stage visualizations (fat burning, ketosis, autophagy zones) based solely on elapsed time, without any actual biomarker data. Users and reviewers question the accuracy.

> *"I entered my catabolic zone four to 16 hours into fasting, meaning my primary fuel source was glucose from liver glycogen and fatty acids from fat stores. (Or so the app said.)"*
> — Fortune magazine review (skeptical tone)

> *"By looking at the length of your fast, and taking in other health factors... Zero can give you a rough guess at the stage of the fast you are in."*
> — TechRadar review (emphasis on "rough guess")

A JMIR study evaluating fasting apps concluded:

> *"Limited clinical input may undermine the evidence-based accuracy and long-term applicability of some apps."*
> — JMIR mHealth and uHealth, 2025 academic study

**Apps cited:** Zero, Fastic, DoFasting, nearly all apps with "body status" features

---

#### #10. Inaccurate Food Database / Bad Calorie Data
**Severity: Medium**

Apps that bundle calorie tracking alongside fasting suffer from incomplete or user-submitted food databases with frequent errors.

> *"It's a very good App, although a lot of food data introduced by users are not reviewed so they can show mistakes."*
> — Fastic, Trustpilot review

> *"One improvement I'd suggest is expanding the food database for calorie entries; it's tricky to find specific items like a hamburger patty without cheese or a bun through a simple search."*
> — DoFasting, Trustpilot review

> *"The biggest problem is the food journal. The food database is terrible and when I try to add my own foods, most of the time I put in all the info only for it to disappear when I press the check mark."*
> — Fastin, Google Play review

**Apps cited:** Fastic, DoFasting, Fastin

### Category 4: Missing Features

#### #11. No Home Screen Widget / Poor Lock Screen Integration
**Severity: Medium-High — One of the most requested features**

Users want at-a-glance fasting progress without opening the app. Widget absence or poor widget implementation is a recurring complaint.

> *"I only wish it stayed as an active notification in the notification bar... being reminded of my progress every time I touch my phone would be such a huge motivation boost 😭🙏🏻 please update to give us this feature."*
> — Easy Fast, App Store review

> *"I would love the feature of a widget that I can add to the home screen. So I can just tap to start or end a fast. Reducing one step seems not much but considering a lot of people will be using it daily, it makes quite a large difference."*
> — Fasting + Intermittent Fasting, App Store review

> *"All the widgets I tried were also locked [behind premium]."*
> — GoFasting, Google Play review

> *"Some things I would add are a home screen widget, and other stages like peak growth hormone and lowest insulin levels."*
> — Easy Fast, App Store review

**Apps cited:** Multiple apps — widgets either missing, broken, or paywalled

---

#### #12. No Data Export / Can't Switch Apps Without Losing History
**Severity: Medium**

Users feel locked into apps because they can't export their fasting history. When an app degrades (price hikes, feature bloat), users face losing months/years of data.

> *"Can I switch from Zero to another app? Yes, but you can't export your history. Start fresh."*
> — The Dolce Way comparative review

> *"The free version... 7-day history is limiting for tracking progress."*
> — The Dolce Way on Zero's free tier

> *"I like looking at all of the badges I had received, the progress I made and my longest streak in anything I've done... Now I'm forced to use another app."*
> — Zero, App Store review (returning user who lost all data)

**Apps cited:** Zero, most apps without export functionality

---

#### #13. No Social/Accountability Features (or Paywalled Ones)
**Severity: Medium**

Users want to fast with friends/family for accountability but either find no social features or discover them locked behind premium.

> *"I think this would really be a value if it included a calorie counter or linked to an app like My Fitness Pal... the connection with the IF FB page is HUGE!!!! Having a community to cheer you on is awesome. Though I don't see why this isn't all linked through the actual app."*
> — BodyFast, App Store review

> *"One feature I would like to see added is an ability to share and view fasting hours/data with family and friends."*
> — Fastyle, App Store review

A 2025 academic study confirmed: apps broadly lack social features, and "developers are encouraged to... incorporate social features to boost user engagement."

**Apps cited:** BodyFast, Fastyle, Zero — the JMIR study noted this as an industry-wide gap

### Category 5: Technical Complaints

#### #14. Bugs After Updates — Broken Streaks, Lost Data, Timer Glitches
**Severity: High**

App updates frequently introduce regressions that break core functionality: timers not recording, streaks resetting, fasts not saving, and data corruption.

> *"I have been trying to log a fast for over 24hrs. I have deleted and redownloaded the app at least 3x. Now I'm forced to use another app."*
> — Zero, App Store review

> *"Since the new update when I end my fast... it will say 'Are you still fasting? You've been fasting for over 20 hours.' It's so frustrating because it's done that 3 times this week and there's no way to correct the mistake."*
> — Fasting For Weight Loss, App Store review

> *"Streaks & milestones are not being earned or incremented. Also, the week view for the Total Fasting Hours chart is not accurate."*
> — Zero, Google Play review

> *"There's some sort of glitch that is showing me I managed to complete a fast on Sunday… in the future."*
> — Fasting + Intermittent Fasting, App Store review

> *"As of today's update, a bug appears where you can't edit daily weight data. A keyboard shows up but you can't backspace or anything."*
> — Easy Fast, App Store review (later fixed)

**Apps cited:** Zero, Fasting For Weight Loss, Fasting+, Easy Fast

---

#### #15. App Lag, Slow Performance, and Crashes
**Severity: Medium**

Users report lag between screens (especially in free tiers where ad frameworks add overhead), slow load times, and app freezes — particularly on older devices.

> *"The only problem I have with it is that it is sometimes very laggy going between screens and when first opening the app. I paid for the ad-free version and I feel like maybe this is where the ads would normally be?"*
> — Fasting + Intermittent Fasting, App Store review

> *"This was my favorite fasting app, but it quit working very suddenly and I couldn't re-download one that would work or anything. So I had to take it from five stars down to two stars."*
> — Easy Fast, App Store review

> *"Then when I push the button a bunch of Chinese writing comes on the screen. Please go back to the original platform!"*
> — Fasting For Weight Loss, App Store review (after update)

**Apps cited:** Fasting+, Easy Fast (isolated), Fasting For Weight Loss

## Key Insight: What Users Actually Want

The most-loved apps in the space (Easy Fast, FastHabit, Fasty) share these traits:
- **No ads, no mandatory subscriptions** — free core features, optional tip/donation or one-time purchase
- **No account required** — open the app, tap start, done
- **Clean, minimal UI** — a timer, weight tracking, maybe water. Nothing else.
- **Privacy-first** — no data collection, no third-party tracking
- **Reliable widgets** — home screen and lock screen glanceable progress
- **Responsive solo developers** — fast bug fixes, community-driven roadmaps

As one user put it:
> *"I built Easy Fast because I was tired of bloated apps that require logins just to set a timer."*
