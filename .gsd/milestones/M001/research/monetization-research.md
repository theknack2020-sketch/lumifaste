## 2. Free vs Paid Feature Splits

### What's Typically Free

- Basic fasting timer (start/stop/countdown)
- 1–3 preset fasting schedules (16:8, 18:6)
- Simple streak tracking
- Basic weight logging
- Limited fasting history (e.g., last 7–10 days)
- A few educational articles
- Apple Health / Google Fit sync (varies)

### What's Typically Premium

- **Personalized fasting plans** (AI-generated or quiz-based schedules)
- **Advanced statistics** (weekly/monthly/yearly trends, correlation charts)
- **Full fasting history** (Zero gates monthly/yearly history behind Plus)
- **Meal plans & recipes** (Fastic 400+ recipes, DoFasting 5,000+ recipes)
- **AI coaching / chatbot** (Simple's AI coach, Fastic's "Fasty")
- **Workout libraries** (DoFasting, BetterMe)
- **Nutrition/calorie/macro tracking** (detailed food logging)
- **Mood & body tracking** (beyond basic weight)
- **Ad-free experience**
- **Community features** (challenges, social)
- **Progress photos / photo diary**
- **Fasting stage science visuals** (what's happening in your body)
- **Custom fasting schedules** (beyond presets)
- **Wearable integrations** (Apple Watch complications, widget support)

### The Paywall Spectrum

| Strategy | Example Apps | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Generous free tier** | Zero, LIFE, Fasted | Timer + basic tracking free; stats/coaching paid |
| **Moderate free tier** | Simple, BodyFast, Fastic | Timer free; plans, recipes, AI locked |
| **Hard paywall** | DoFasting | No free version; quiz → paywall |
| **One-time purchase** | FastHabit, Fastient | Low-cost unlock; no recurring revenue |

## 4. Churn Rates for Fasting/Health App Subscriptions

### By Plan Duration

| Plan Type | First Renewal Churn | Year 1 Retention |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** | 30–50% | Steepest YoY decline (−27%) |
| **Monthly** | 15–40% | Moderate |
| **Annual** | Lower first-renewal churn | Upper quartile retain 60–75% |

### Key Churn Data Points

- **Day 30 retention** for H&F apps: ~3% (all users, not just subscribers)
- **Annual subscription retention rate:** 33% (H&F category average)
- **46% of users** cancel via app store within the first billing cycle.
- **44% of cancellations** happen within the first 90 days.
- **2–5% of payers** will claim a refund (higher in Health & Fitness and Education than other categories).
- Weekly plans churn **3x faster** than monthly plans.
- Annual plans **reduce churn by 51%** compared to monthly plans.
- **Price increases** lead to a ~15% immediate spike in churn.
- The first renewal is the **biggest churn event** — retention improves significantly after surviving it.
- Apps offering **"pause subscription"** reduce cancellations by 18%.
- Apps with **community features** reduce churn by 23%.

### Fasting-Specific Churn Factors

- Fasting apps face seasonal churn: high sign-ups in January (New Year's resolutions), significant drop-off by March.
- 30% of Americans who set fitness goals don't complete them.
- Habit-forming features, personalization, and community engagement are the strongest churn-reduction levers.

## 6. Pricing Psychology for Health Apps

### Anchoring

- **Show the monthly price first, then present annual as the deal.** Users anchor on the higher monthly cost, making the annual price feel like a steal.
- Example: "$9.99/mo" crossed out → "Just $4.17/mo billed annually" (Zero's approach).
- Premium pricing ($9.99/mo+) actually shows **higher** download-to-paying conversion (2.66% vs 1.49% for low-priced apps) — perceived value matters.

### Trial Psychology

- **$1 introductory trials** boost conversion by 38%.
- Longer trials (17–32 days) yield the highest conversion rates (45.7% median). Users need time to form habits.
- 7-day free trials are the industry default but carry highest Day 0–1 cancellation rates (preemptive cancellation).
- **Pay-as-you-go introductory offers** (e.g., $0.99/mo for 3 months) can reduce sticker shock while still generating revenue during the trial period.

### Discount Framing

- **59% of mobile subscribers** prefer annual plans when offered a 30–40% discount.
- Present savings as a percentage AND absolute amount: "Save 58% — that's $50/year."
- Use strikethrough pricing on the monthly rate next to annual to reinforce the discount.

### Behavioral Triggers

- **Loss aversion:** After a trial, users have tracked data they don't want to lose. Gate access to fasting history behind premium.
- **Personalization investment:** Quiz → personalized plan creates sunk-cost feeling. Users are 28% more likely to convert after personalization.
- **Social proof:** "Join 10M+ fasters" (Zero) or showing community size reduces friction.
- **Seasonal urgency:** Q5 (Dec 26–Jan) sees the highest subscription intent. Lower ad costs + peak motivation = best conversion window.
- **Goal-oriented segments** (e.g., weight loss, longevity, metabolic health) have significantly higher retention and conversion than broad targeting.

### Paywall Timing

- **Hard paywalls** (requiring payment before any use) show 12.11% median conversion vs 2.18% for freemium — but risk higher churn if users feel locked in too early.
- **Soft paywall after value demonstration** (e.g., after completing first fast, after seeing fasting stages visualization) is the most common pattern in successful fasting apps.
- The quiz-to-paywall funnel (Simple, DoFasting) creates personalization investment before showing the price.

### Pricing Tier Strategy

- **Two-tier approach:** Monthly and Annual (most fasting apps use this)
- **Three-tier with decoy:** Monthly / Quarterly / Annual, where Quarterly is priced to make Annual look best (anchoring decoy)
- 61% of subscription companies offer at least three pricing tiers.
- Annual subscribers are **2.4x more profitable** than monthly subscribers.

## 7. StoreKit 2 Subscription Best Practices

### Offer Types Available

Apple provides four distinct offer types, all configurable in App Store Connect:

| Offer Type | Target Audience | Eligibility | Redemption Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Introductory Offers** | New subscribers | Never subscribed in the subscription group | One per subscription group |
| **Promotional Offers** | Current or lapsed subscribers | Must have previously subscribed | Unlimited (you control) |
| **Offer Codes** | Anyone (acquisition, retention, win-back) | Configurable per code | One-time-use or custom codes |
| **Win-Back Offers** (iOS 18+) | Churned subscribers | Automatically determined by App Store | Configurable |

### Pricing Options (for all offer types)

1. **Free Trial** — User pays nothing for the offer duration. Most common for introductory offers.
2. **Pay As You Go** — Reduced price per billing period for a set number of periods (e.g., $0.99/mo for 3 months).
3. **Pay Up Front** — One-time reduced payment for the entire offer duration (e.g., $3.99 for 6 months).

### Recommended Trial Strategy for a Fasting App

**Primary recommendation: 7-day free trial on annual plan**
- Industry standard that users understand
- Annual plan reduces churn by 51% vs monthly
- After Year 1 of paid service, Apple's commission drops from 30% to 15% (or 15% → 0% under Small Business Program)

**Alternative: 14–30 day trial**
- Data shows 17–32 day trials have the highest conversion (45.7%)
- Gives users 2–4 full fasting cycles to build the habit
- Risk: higher upfront cost (longer free period)

**Consider also: Pay-as-you-go introductory offer**
- e.g., $0.99/mo for first 3 months, then $4.99/mo
- Generates revenue during intro period
- Reduces sticker shock for price-sensitive markets

### StoreKit 2 Implementation Notes

- **Eligibility checking:** StoreKit 2 provides `Product.SubscriptionInfo.isEligibleForIntroOffer` — more reliable than StoreKit 1's receipt-based approach. Always check before displaying offers.
- **SubscriptionStoreView:** Built-in SwiftUI view for subscription management. Use `.preferredSubscriptionOffer` modifier to prioritize which offer to show.
- **Transaction.offer:** Populated in iOS 17.2+ when an offer is redeemed. Contains `id`, `type` (introductory/promotional/winBack), and `paymentMode`.
- **RenewalInfo.offer:** Available in iOS 18.0+, shows if the next renewal includes an offer.
- **Win-Back Offers (iOS 18+):** Automatically surfaced via StoreKit Message API. Your app receives a `winBackOffer` message when eligible. The offer sheet presents automatically — you just need an active transaction observer.
- **App Store Server Notifications V2:** Enable these for real-time updates on renewals, cancellations, billing issues, and refunds. This is the recommended approach over polling.
- **Product identifiers from server:** Serve product IDs from a backend API rather than hardcoding. This enables paywall A/B testing without app updates.

### Promotional Offer Strategy (Churn Reduction)

- Trigger a promotional offer when a user shows **cancellation intent** (e.g., navigates to subscription management).
- Use server-side logic to determine eligibility and generate JWS signatures.
- Common pattern: "Stay for 50% off your next 3 months" at the point of cancellation.
- Customers can redeem promotional offers multiple times (unlike introductory offers).

### Win-Back Offer Strategy (iOS 18+)

- Configure in App Store Connect with priority levels (High/Normal).
- Offers can appear in the App Store itself, even if users haven't opened your app.
- Combine with email campaigns using Offer Codes for maximum re-engagement reach.
- Set up offers at varying discount depths: light discount for recent churners, deeper discount for long-lapsed users.

### Revenue Optimization

- **Small Business Program:** If eligible (< $1M annual revenue), Apple takes only 15% commission (vs 30%). After 1 year of paid service per subscriber, commission drops to 15% regardless.
- **Grace period:** Enable billing grace period (6 or 16 days) to recover involuntary churn from payment failures. Smart dunning recovery tools recover ~37% of failed charges.
- **Subscription groups:** Place all plan tiers in one group. Upgrades take effect immediately; downgrades at next renewal. One introductory offer per group per user.

## Summary: Recommended Monetization Strategy for a New Fasting App

### Pricing

| Plan | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly | $9.99/mo | Anchoring price — makes annual look compelling |
| Annual | $49.99/yr (~$4.17/mo) | Primary revenue driver, 58% savings vs monthly |
| Lifetime | $99.99 (optional) | Consider offering later once LTV is understood |

### Free Tier

Give away enough to build the habit:
- Fasting timer with 2–3 preset schedules
- Basic streak tracking
- 7-day fasting history
- Limited educational content

Gate the value-add features:
- Full history & statistics
- Personalized plans
- AI coaching / insights
- Meal suggestions / recipes
- Advanced body metrics
- Ad-free experience

### Conversion Funnel

1. **Onboarding quiz** → personalized plan (creates investment)
2. **Free trial: 7 days** on annual plan (or test 14-day)
3. **Soft paywall** after first completed fast or after seeing personalized insights
4. **Promotional offer** on cancellation intent (50% off 3 months)
5. **Win-back offer** (iOS 18+) for churned users
6. **Seasonal campaigns** (Q5 / New Year's push)

### Target Benchmarks

- Download-to-trial: 15–25%
- Trial-to-paid: 40–50% (aim for top quartile at 60%+)
- Annual plan churn: < 40% first year (top quartile: 25%)
- Revenue per install (Day 60): > $0.63 (H&F median)
