# Fasting App UX/UI Benchmark Analysis

> Competitive analysis of the top 8 fasting apps: Zero, Fastic, Simple, BodyFast, Life Fasting Tracker, YAZIO, Ate, Window.
> Research date: March 2026

---

## 1. Zero (Zero Longevity)

**Overview:** One of the earliest and most recognized fasting apps. Science-driven, clean design, recently expanded beyond fasting into nutrition tracking with Zero Plus subscription ($69.99/yr).

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Dark backgrounds with green/teal accent colors. Calming, clinical aesthetic.
- **Identity:** Minimalist, science-forward brand. Uses the "Four Pillars of Health" color system — each pillar (fasting, nutrition, activity, sleep) gets a distinct color in the "Loop" visualization.
- **Day/Night mode:** Timer colors shift depending on time of day — warm tones during day, cool/dark at night.

### Timer Screen
- **Circular timer** is the centerpiece — large, prominent ring that fills as fasting progresses.
- "Start a fast with a single tap" — minimal friction to begin.
- Supports both count-up and count-down modes (users report preferring count-up to avoid clock-watching).
- Green progress circles at top show weekly streak/completion.
- Fasting stages (fat burning, ketosis, autophagy) shown along the timer progression.

### Onboarding Flow
- Initial questionnaire covers fasting goals, experience level, preferred fasting method (16:8, 18:6, etc.).
- Relatively short compared to competitors — focused on getting users to the timer quickly.
- Account creation required for syncing.
- Educational content woven into early experience via articles and videos.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- **"Today" tab** is the primary view — shows the Loop (multi-colored activity ring), fasting timer, and habit tracker.
- "+" icon in upper right for logging meals, workouts, etc.
- **"Me" tab** provides calendar view of progress, weekly metrics, trends.
- Metrics tracked: total fasting hours, weight, active minutes, sleep hours, resting heart rate, caloric intake, glucose levels, ketone levels.

### Navigation
- **Bottom tab bar** with core sections: Today, Timer, Learn, Me.
- Clean, standard iOS/Android navigation pattern.

### Data Visualization
- Calendar view with colored indicators for fasting completion.
- Loop visualization (Apple Watch-inspired activity rings) for the Four Pillars.
- Weekly averages and trend charts for weight, fasting hours.
- Streak tracking with badges and achievements.

### Premium Upsell
- Free tier is genuinely generous — timer, journal, mood tracking, articles all free.
- Zero Plus ($69.99/yr) gates: nutrition/meal tracking, detailed insights, advanced stats, personalized recommendations.
- **Non-aggressive approach** — does not pressure users to upgrade. Praised by Fortune as the best free fasting app.
- Upsell surfaces contextually when users try to access Plus features.

### Dark Mode
- Yes — automatic day/night color shifting is a core design feature.

### Accessibility
- Apple Watch support with fast start/stop from wrist.
- Apple Health integration for syncing weight, activity, sleep.
- Recent updates mention "Added Accessibility Features" (v6.0).
- Live Activity widgets and Dynamic Island support on iOS.

## 2. Fastic

**Overview:** Lifestyle-oriented fasting app with 26M+ users. Emphasizes community, education, and holistic wellness (hydration, exercise, sleep). Strong onboarding praised industry-wide.

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Vibrant, warm, friendly — greens, oranges, and soft pastels. Playful illustrations.
- **Identity:** Approachable, coaching-oriented. "Fun" and energetic compared to Zero's clinical feel.
- Mascot/character-driven UI elements in educational content.

### Timer Screen
- **Circular timer** with fasting phase indicators (anabolic, catabolic, fat burning, ketosis, autophagy).
- Phase-by-phase progress visualization with color-coded segments.
- Hydration tracker integrated alongside the timer.

### Onboarding Flow
- **Extensive and highly praised** — one of the best onboarding flows in the health app space.
- Asks "lots of questions" — current weight, target weight, height, experience level, medical conditions, lifestyle.
- **Key differentiators:**
  - Benefits-oriented opening screens for motivation.
  - Regular pauses between questions to set expectations and reinforce IF benefits.
  - Educational tidbits interspersed throughout (not just data collection).
  - Transparency about medical risks if conditions flagged.
  - Showcases health experts behind the app (credibility building).
  - Warm, friendly copywriting throughout.
  - Progress indicator showing completion percentage.
- **Paywall placement:** End of onboarding, after full personalization. Shows personalized results with expected timeline. Considered a best-in-class paywall design.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- Multi-section dashboard: timer, daily tasks, water intake, step counter, educational content feed.
- "Personal Feed" with fasting hacks, nutrition advice, motivational content.
- Community challenges and social feed visible from home.

### Navigation
- **Bottom tab bar** — Home, Timer, Community, Recipes, Profile.
- More tabs than Zero reflecting broader feature scope.

### Data Visualization
- Progress charts for weight, fasting consistency.
- Challenge completion tracking.
- Community leaderboards.

### Premium Upsell
- Fastic Plus (~$39.99/yr) — meal plans, personalized coaching, full recipe library, advanced community features.
- Paywall appears post-onboarding with personalized promise — shows predicted results and timeline.
- Community challenges available in free tier to hook users.

### Dark Mode
- Supported.

### Accessibility
- Available in multiple languages.
- Step tracking integration.

## 3. Simple (Simple.Life)

**Overview:** AI coaching-forward fasting app with 5M+ downloads. Positions as a weight-loss-first tool rather than pure fasting tracker. Heavy investment in personalization and guided support.

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Clean whites, soft blues and greens. Professional, medical-adjacent aesthetic.
- **Identity:** "Smart" and trustworthy. Feels more like a health coaching platform than a timer.

### Timer Screen
- **Circular timer** with clear fasting/eating window visualization.
- Integrated metabolic phase indicators.

### Onboarding Flow
- **Detailed, quiz-heavy onboarding** — asks extensive questions for personalization.
- AI-powered recommendations generated from responses.
- Ongoing check-ins post-onboarding refine recommendations over time.
- Nutritionist-guided content helps users understand "why" not just "what."
- Described as "truly adaptive" in 2026 updates.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- AI coach prominent on dashboard — can answer questions and offer encouragement.
- Clean, polished interface.
- Daily task recommendations.

### Navigation
- **Bottom tab bar** — standard mobile pattern.

### Data Visualization
- Weight tracking with trend analysis.
- Fasting completion history.
- AI-generated insights about patterns.

### Premium Upsell
- **Leans hard into premium** — among the more expensive options ($10–20/month or discounted annually).
- Free tier is "Limited" compared to competitors.
- AI coaching is the primary premium draw.
- Some users report aggressive upselling.

### Dark Mode
- Supported.

### Accessibility
- AI coach provides accessible, conversational interface for beginners.
- Educational content with nutritionist backing.

## 4. BodyFast

**Overview:** German-made app, 50M+ installs, #1 in 48 countries. Revenue driven by "Coach" feature. Emphasis on plan variety and gamification.

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Blue-dominant with white backgrounds. Clean, structured, European design sensibility.
- **Identity:** Structured, plan-oriented. Feels more "program" than "tool."

### Timer Screen
- **Circular countdown timer** with notifications/reminders.
- Fasting stages visualization showing biological processes during fast.
- "Joker Day" system allows planned breaks from fasting.

### Onboarding Flow
- Goal-setting focused — target weight, progress tracking from day one.
- Coach feature calculates optimal fasting plan weekly.
- Less elaborate than Fastic's onboarding but functional.
- Free plans available immediately; Coach subscription pitched for optimization.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- Weight and body measurements displayed in interactive graphs.
- Water tracker with reminders.
- Weekly challenges section.
- Personal feed with expert tips, nutrition advice, motivational content.
- Trophy/achievement collection visible.

### Navigation
- **Bottom tab bar** — standard pattern.
- Well-organized settings menus.

### Data Visualization
- Interactive weight and body measurement graphs.
- Weekly challenge completion tracking.
- Trophy system and streak visualization.
- Progress statistics over time.

### Premium Upsell
- **Coach subscription** (~$10-15/month or ~$49.99/yr) — the primary monetization lever.
- Free version: 10+ customizable fasting plans, timer, weight tracker, fasting stages, water tracker.
- Coach unlocks: personalized weekly plans, daily coaching, 100+ recipes, food facts, weekly challenges, SOS expert help, all fasting plans, Joker Days.
- Upgrade prompts throughout but not as aggressive as some competitors.
- No ads in free version — noted as a positive differentiator.

### Dark Mode
- Available.

### Accessibility
- Google Fit integration.
- Multi-language support.
- Timer includes reminder notifications.

## 5. Life Fasting Tracker

**Overview:** One of the original fasting apps with open-source roots. Known for social "Fasting Circles" and no-frills reliability. Community-first approach.

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Simpler color scheme, blues and greens. More utilitarian than polished.
- **Identity:** Community-driven, straightforward, "honest tool" vibe.
- Design has not kept pace with newer competitors — described as "dated UI."

### Timer Screen
- Functional countdown timer.
- Basic but reliable.
- Less visual flair than Zero or Fastic.

### Onboarding Flow
- **Minimal onboarding** — no mandatory account creation.
- Quick start to fasting.
- Custom schedule setup.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- Current stats, fasting calendar view, basic statistics.
- Fasting Circles (group fasting) prominently featured.
- Research-based articles and learning library — free, reviewed quarterly by scientists.

### Navigation
- **Bottom tab bar** — simpler structure.

### Data Visualization
- Fasting calendar views.
- Basic statistics and completion tracking.
- Ketone tracking support.
- Weight tracking absent in free tier.

### Premium Upsell
- **Generous free tier** — learning library, basic tracking all free.
- Premium adds deeper features but core functionality is robust without it.
- No aggressive upselling — recommended in forums for its restraint.

### Dark Mode
- Limited/basic support.

### Accessibility
- Lightweight, works on older devices.
- No mandatory account — privacy-friendly.
- Open-source background appeals to tech-savvy users.

## 6. YAZIO

**Overview:** Primarily a calorie/macro tracker that added fasting as a feature. Strong food database, AI photo meal logging. ~$39.99/yr for Pro. More of a nutrition app with fasting support than a dedicated fasting app.

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Colorful "pastel rings" — green, orange, yellow progress indicators. Friendly, data-rich aesthetic.
- **Identity:** Data-driven nutrition tracker. Charming, detailed, information-dense.

### Timer Screen
- **Linear/visual timer** for fasting windows — integrated into the broader nutrition dashboard.
- Select fasting method (16:8 recommended), tap "Start Tracker."
- Less prominent than dedicated fasting apps — fasting is one tab among several.

### Onboarding Flow
- Comprehensive survey: goals, activity level, physical stats, past challenges, weekend eating habits, IF preferences.
- "Refreshingly straightforward" — takes just a few minutes.
- Sprinkles helpful tips and realistic expectations throughout.
- Post-onboarding paywall presents 12-month or 3-month plans — can appear as if there's no free option.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- Calorie/macro tracking is primary — daily calorie target, macronutrient breakdown (protein, carbs, fats).
- Meal logging by search, barcode scan, AI photo, recent items, or favorites.
- Water intake tracker.
- Fasting timer on dedicated tab.
- Nutritional info for each meal easily viewable.

### Navigation
- **Bottom tab bar** — Diary, Fasting, Recipes, Progress, More.

### Data Visualization
- Detailed calorie and macro breakdown charts.
- Weight tracking with trend lines.
- "Smart food rating system" in Pro.
- Estimation of weeks remaining to goal weight.
- Progress over time with intuitive graphs.

### Premium Upsell
- **Aggressive for free version** — frequent lengthy video ads reported. Some users describe it as "utterly grifty."
- Frequent "85% off BUT ONLY FOR ONE MORE HOUR" promotions — false urgency tactics.
- Pro unlocks: barcode scan, macro goals, recipes, detailed analytics, ad-free experience.
- Post-onboarding paywall makes free option hard to find.
- No community features or personalized coaching — accountability rated 2/5 by testers.

### Dark Mode
- Supported.

### Accessibility
- AI photo meal logging reduces manual entry burden.
- Apple Health, Google Fit integration.
- Multiple logging options (search, scan, photo, recent, favorites).
- Smart reminders for meals, hydration, fasting windows — "without becoming intrusive."

## 7. Ate (Ate Food Journal)

**Overview:** Not a traditional fasting app — it's a visual food journaling tool that complements fasting habits. Mindful eating focus. Photo-based meal logging with time tracking.

### Color Scheme & Visual Identity
- **Palette:** Warm, organic tones. Earth colors, soft gradients.
- **Identity:** Mindful, intentional, anti-diet-culture positioning. Feels more like a wellness journal than a tracking tool.

### Timer Screen
- **No traditional circular timer.** Time tracking is embedded in the food journal — tracks when meals happen relative to eating/fasting windows.
- Visual timeline of eating events rather than countdown.

### Onboarding Flow
- Minimal — focuses on setting up the photo journal.
- Goal framing around mindfulness and understanding eating patterns rather than weight loss.

### Dashboard / Home Screen
- **Photo-based timeline** — visual food diary as the primary interface.
- Each meal captured as a photo with timestamp.
- "On path" vs "off path" categorization of meals.
- Time-between-meals visualization.

### Navigation
- Simple tab structure — Journal, Insights, Community.

### Data Visualization
- Photo timeline is the primary visualization.
- Eating pattern analysis — when, what, why.
- "On path" vs "off path" trend tracking.
- Minimal charts — philosophy leans toward qualitative rather than quantitative tracking.

### Premium Upsell
- Lighter monetization than competitors.
- Premium unlocks deeper insights and community features.

### Dark Mode
- Basic support.

### Accessibility
- Photo-first approach reduces barrier to logging.
- Minimal text input required.
- Inclusive, non-judgmental language throughout.

## Synthesis: Cross-App Pattern Analysis

### Timer Screen Design (Universal)

| App | Timer Type | Key Differentiator |
|-----|-----------|-------------------|
| Zero | Circular | Day/night color shift, count-up/down toggle |
| Fastic | Circular | Phase-colored segments, hydration integrated |
| Simple | Circular | AI coaching overlay |
| BodyFast | Circular | Joker Day system, stage visualization |
| Life | Circular | Basic, reliable, group fasting |
| YAZIO | Linear/Integrated | Secondary to calorie tracking |
| Ate | Timeline | Photo-based, no traditional timer |
| Window | Simple circular | Minimal, timer-only |

**Verdict:** Circular timers dominate. The circular metaphor maps naturally to time progression and completion percentage. Zero and Fastic have the most polished implementations.

### Onboarding Comparison

| App | Steps | Style | Paywall Placement |
|-----|-------|-------|-------------------|
| Zero | ~8-10 | Quick, goal-focused | After exploration |
| Fastic | ~15-20 | Educational, warm, thorough | End of onboarding (personalized) |
| Simple | ~12-15 | AI-driven, detailed quiz | Post-quiz, prominent |
| BodyFast | ~8-10 | Plan-selection focused | Coach upsell mid-experience |
| Life | ~3-5 | Minimal, optional account | Minimal/contextual |
| YAZIO | ~10-12 | Comprehensive health survey | Post-survey (hard to skip) |
| Ate | ~3-5 | Minimal, journal-focused | Contextual |
| Window | ~2-3 | Near-zero, start immediately | Minimal |

**Verdict:** The market is split between thorough-onboarding apps (Fastic, Simple, YAZIO) and minimal-onboarding apps (Life, Window, Ate). Fastic's approach is the gold standard for conversion — it builds investment, educates, establishes trust, and personalizes the paywall.

### Premium Upsell Strategies

| Strategy | Apps Using It | User Reception |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Generous free tier, gentle upsell** | Zero, Life, Window | Highest user trust and loyalty |
| **Personalized post-onboarding paywall** | Fastic, Simple | High conversion, accepted by users |
| **Visible-but-locked features** | BodyFast, generic fasting apps | Effective but can frustrate |
| **Aggressive ads + false urgency** | YAZIO free tier | Negative user sentiment, "grifty" perception |
| **No free trial, direct subscription** | Some newer apps | Filters for high-intent but loses casual users |

## Best Patterns to Adopt ✅

### 1. Circular Timer as Hero Element
Every successful fasting app centers a circular timer. It's intuitive, motivating, and maps to completion psychology. Zero's day/night color shifting is elegant. Fastic's phase-segmented ring is informative.

### 2. Fastic-Style Onboarding (Invest → Educate → Personalize → Convert)
- Ask meaningful questions that genuinely improve the experience.
- Intersperse educational moments between questions to build trust and reduce fatigue.
- Use warm, conversational copy — not clinical or corporate.
- Show a progress bar to create completion motivation.
- Place the paywall AFTER personalization when user investment is highest.
- Frame subscription as access to "your personalized plan" rather than "premium features."

### 3. Generous Free Tier with Contextual Upgrades
Zero's model wins the most trust. Let users experience the core loop (start fast → track → complete → see stats) for free. Surface premium features in context, not as roadblocks.

### 4. Fasting Stage Visualization
Show what's happening biologically — anabolic state → fat burning → ketosis → autophagy. This educates users, provides motivation during difficult phases, and differentiates from a basic countdown.

### 5. One-Tap Fast Start
The core action must be effortless. Zero's "start a fast with a single tap" is the benchmark. Ate and Window demonstrate that zero-friction start is key.

### 6. Streak & Achievement Systems
Badges, streaks, and trophies work. BodyFast's trophy system and Zero's streak tracking drive retention through reward psychology. Keep it meaningful — don't over-gamify.

### 7. Day/Night Adaptive UI
Zero's automatic color shifting based on time-of-day is both beautiful and functional. Dark mode for nighttime fasting checks reduces eye strain. This should be automatic, not a manual setting.

### 8. Educational Content as Core Feature (Not Gated)
Zero and Life provide free access to science-backed articles and videos. This builds trust, positions the app as authoritative, and reduces churn from confused beginners.

### 9. Multi-Signal Dashboard
The best dashboards show: fasting timer status, streak, recent trend, and one secondary metric (weight or water). Zero's "Loop" concept is clever but don't overload — clarity beats comprehensiveness.

### 10. Apple Watch / Widget / Dynamic Island Support
Zero's "end your fast from the watch" feature is loved by users. Lock screen widgets and Dynamic Island keep fasting visible without opening the app — reduces friction and increases engagement.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid ❌

### 1. YAZIO-Style Aggressive Monetization
Lengthy video ads, "85% off for ONE HOUR ONLY" fake urgency, and hiding basic features (barcode scan, macro goals) behind the paywall creates hostile user sentiment. Multiple users describe YAZIO's free experience as "utterly grifty."

### 2. Paywall Before Value
Presenting a subscription screen before the user has completed a single fast is a conversion-killer for long-term retention. Let users experience the "aha moment" first.

### 3. Over-Long Onboarding Without Payoff
17+ step onboarding (like some generic fasting apps) only works if every question visibly improves the experience. Asking questions just to profile users without demonstrating value causes drop-off. Fastic succeeds because each question feels purposeful.

### 4. Feature Bloat Masquerading as Value
Fastic's attempt to be a social network + meal planner + exercise tracker + sleep logger creates complexity. Users who want a fasting timer don't want to navigate past recipes and community feeds. The trend in 2025-2026 is toward minimalism.

### 5. Dated/Static UI
Life Fasting Tracker's "dated UI" is noted by multiple reviewers as a competitive disadvantage despite strong functionality. Visual design matters for perceived quality and motivation — health apps need to feel fresh.

### 6. Invisible Free Option
YAZIO's post-onboarding paywall that presents only 12-month and 3-month plans, making it "appear as if there's no free option," erodes trust immediately. Always make the free path clear and accessible.

### 7. Breaking Core Features in Updates
Zero's Play Store reviews show significant backlash after UI redesigns that changed familiar timer behavior: "I also wish the previous timer would come back. Weekly averages were clearer as well." Preserve muscle memory. Iterate, don't revolutionize.

### 8. Ignoring Count-Up Timer Preference
Zero users report preferring count-up timers because "counting down makes me want to eat as soon as it hits 0:00." Offer both, but consider count-up as default — it frames fasting as achievement rather than deprivation.

### 9. Mandatory Account Creation
Life Fasting Tracker's no-account-required approach is loved. Forcing account creation before using a timer is unnecessary friction. Let users fast first, prompt account creation when they have data worth saving.

### 10. Medical Disclaimers as Afterthought
Fastic gets this right — asks about medical conditions during onboarding and transparently warns about risks. Apps that bury disclaimers in settings or ToS miss an opportunity to build trust and demonstrate genuine user care.

---

## Recommended Design Principles for a New Fasting App

Based on this analysis, the optimal approach synthesizes:

1. **Zero's restraint** — Clean, calming UI with a generous free tier
2. **Fastic's onboarding** — Educational, trust-building, personalized
3. **BodyFast's gamification** — Streaks, trophies, weekly challenges (light touch)
4. **Ate's mindfulness** — Non-judgmental language, focus on patterns over punishment
5. **Window's speed** — One-tap start, zero friction to begin fasting
6. **Life's transparency** — No mandatory account, open approach

And avoids:
- YAZIO's aggressive monetization and fake urgency
- Feature bloat that dilutes the core fasting experience
- Paywall-before-value sequences
- Dated design that undermines perceived quality
